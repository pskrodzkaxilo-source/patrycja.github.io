Part I: The Beginning The Sunken Archive of Oakhaven was a tomb of forgotten knowledge, submerged beneath the labyrinthine sewers of a corrupt metropolis. The water here was stagnant, ankle-deep, and possessed a pitch-black, glassy quality that perfectly mirrored the vaulted, rotting ceilings above. Towering stone statues of ancient scholars, their faces eroded by centuries of dampness, stood as silent sentinels in the gloom. They held aloft massive bronze braziers, but the fire within them was not natural; it was a cold, flickering blue flame that cast unnaturally long, twitching shadows across the flooded catacombs. The air was suffocatingly thick, heavy with the scent of mildew, decaying parchment, and the sharp, metallic tang of ozone that always accompanied lingering, illegal magic. Hidden within the deepest recesses of this subterranean market, Niamh Corvus—known only as Moth—crouched atop a fallen, moss-draped pillar. She was a creature defined by the dark, clad in layers of frayed, mismatched silk that absorbed the dim light. Her eyes were solid, polished obsidian, devoid of whites or irises, constantly scanning the rippling reflections in the water below. Yet, it was her skin that told her tragic story. Every visible inch of her face, neck, and arms was obscured by a frantic, overlapping tapestry of dark ink. These tattoos were not decorative; they were a desperate ledger. Names, crude sketches of faces, recipes, and fragments of street maps were etched into her flesh. Moth was a Shadow-Weaver. She could pull the darkness apart and spin it into physical form, but the magic demanded a cruel tithe. With every spell cast, the magic reached into her mind and devoured a random memory, erasing it forever. The ink on her skin was her only defense against the encroaching void in her head. She spent her days tracing the names, trying to force herself to remember people she no longer recognized. A heavy, deliberate splash broke the oppressive silence of the cavern. Moth melted seamlessly into the deeper shadows of the pillar, her dark eyes tracking the disturbance. Two figures waded through the flooded aisles of ruined bookshelves. The first was a lean, weathered man whose hair had turned prematurely stark white from extreme stress. He wore the tattered, unrecognizable remnants of a high-ranking Inquisition uniform, stripped of its insignias and stained with dried mud. His right arm, from the elbow down, was encased in a heavy, runic gauntlet of charred iron. This was Kaelen, the disgraced swordsman. Beside him moved a true giant. Oric towered nearly seven feet tall, possessing a frame built of thick, dense muscle wrapped in loose, coarse linen robes. However, his imposing size was betrayed by his painstakingly slow, gentle movements. He stepped through the water as if afraid of waking a sleeping child. The reason for his caution was visible on his skin. Thick patches of dull, grey basalt crusted over his neck and forearms, fusing with his flesh. Hanging carefully from his broad leather belt was a fragile glass terrarium containing a single, vibrant blue butterfly. "My heart rate is climbing. The dampness... it makes the joints stiffen," Oric’s voice rumbled, so soft it barely disturbed the air, his eyes locked entirely on the gentle fluttering of the butterfly's wings. "Keep breathing. Keep watching the wings," Kaelen replied, his tone tight, his jaw clenched as he cradled his heavy gauntlet. Inside the metal glove rested the Sun-Brand, a cursed blade that physically burned his hand every second it was drawn. Moth observed the heavy, grey stone creeping up Oric’s jawline. She recognized the affliction immediately: the Curse of the Stone Heart. If the giant’s heart beat too fast from fear, anger, or exertion, his blood would thicken, and his body would calcify into an unyielding statue. He was forced by his own biology to be an absolute pacifist. Slipping down from the pillar, Moth materialized in front of them, her silk garments making no sound. She did not ask for their names or their money. She simply pointed to her own inked temple, offering them the location of the map they desperately sought—the map to the Cradle of the World-Spirit, a mythical geyser of pure life energy said to cure any curse. In exchange, she demanded passage with them to heal her own fractured mind. Before Kaelen could utter a word of protest, the cavern was bathed in a blinding, aggressive golden light. The blue flames of the braziers were instantly swallowed by the harsh illumination of sun-crystal lanterns. Five heavily armored Inquisitors waded violently into the archive, their heavy repeating crossbows raised and locked onto Kaelen. The golden light struck the shadows, forcing them to retreat, exposing the three fugitives. The sudden, booming shout of the lead Inquisitor commanding their surrender echoed like a thunderclap against the stone walls. The spike of pure terror was instantaneous. Oric gasped, his massive chest heaving. The stone on his neck visibly cracked and expanded, creeping violently over his collarbone as his heart hammered against his ribs in absolute panic. There was no time for negotiation or combat. Moth threw herself forward, her hands plunging straight into the deepest pool of black water beneath the golden light. She screamed, not from physical pain, but from the agonizing sensation of a memory being ripped from her consciousness. She felt the threads of shadow beneath the water, grabbed them with her mind, and pulled with all her might. The shadows solidified into thick, iron-hard cables that wrapped around the foundational pillars of the archive. With a deafening, grinding crunch, Moth tore the pillars down. The vaulted stone ceiling collapsed in a catastrophic cascade of rubble and black water, completely sealing the passageway between the fugitives and the Inquisition. As the dust settled in the newly formed darkness of an escape tunnel, Moth collapsed onto her knees in the wet dirt. She frantically rolled up her damp sleeves, her trembling fingers tracing a name tattooed on her wrist. She read the word "sister" over and over again, weeping bitterly as she realized the face that belonged to the word was completely, permanently gone. Looking down at her devastated form, Kaelen and Oric silently swore an oath to protect her, binding the three broken souls together.
