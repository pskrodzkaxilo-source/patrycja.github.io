Part I: The Sunken Archive

Introduction Chapter 1: The Wastes Conclusion

The Sunken Archive of Oakhaven was a tomb of forgotten knowledge, submerged beneath the labyrinthine sewers of a corrupt metropolis. The water here was stagnant, ankle-deep, and possessed a pitch-black, glassy quality that perfectly mirrored the vaulted, rotting ceilings above. Towering stone statues of ancient scholars, their faces eroded by centuries of dampness, stood as silent sentinels in the gloom. They held aloft massive bronze braziers, but the fire within them was not natural; it was a cold, flickering blue flame that cast unnaturally long, twitching shadows across the flooded catacombs.

Hidden within the deepest recesses of this subterranean market, Niamh Corvus—known only as Moth—crouched atop a fallen, moss-draped pillar. She was a creature defined by the dark, clad in layers of frayed, mismatched silk that absorbed the dim light. Her eyes were solid, polished obsidian, devoid of whites or irises, constantly scanning the rippling reflections in the water below. Yet, it was her skin that told her tragic story. Every visible inch of her face, neck, and arms was obscured by a frantic, overlapping tapestry of dark ink. These tattoos were not decorative; they were a desperate ledger. Names, crude sketches of faces, recipes, and fragments of street maps were etched into her flesh.

Moth was a Shadow-Weaver. She could pull the darkness apart and spin it into physical form, but the magic demanded a cruel tithe. With every spell cast, the magic reached into her mind and devoured a random memory, erasing it forever. The ink on her skin was her only defense against the encroaching void in her head.

A heavy, deliberate splash broke the oppressive silence of the cavern. Moth melted seamlessly into the deeper shadows of the pillar, her dark eyes tracking the disturbance. Two figures waded through the flooded aisles of ruined bookshelves. The first was a lean, weathered man whose hair had turned prematurely stark white from extreme stress. His right arm, from the elbow down, was encased in a heavy, runic gauntlet of charred iron. This was Kaelen, the disgraced swordsman.

Beside him moved a true giant. Oric towered nearly seven feet tall, possessing a frame built of thick, dense muscle wrapped in loose, coarse linen robes. Thick patches of dull, grey basalt crusted over his neck and forearms, fusing with his flesh. Hanging carefully from his broad leather belt was a fragile glass terrarium containing a single, vibrant blue butterfly.

"My heart rate is climbing. The dampness... it makes the joints stiffen," Oric’s voice rumbled, so soft it barely disturbed the air. "Keep breathing. Keep watching the wings," Kaelen replied, his tone tight, his jaw clenched as he cradled his heavy gauntlet.

Moth slipped down from the pillar, materializing in front of them. She offered them the location of the map they desperately sought—the map to the Cradle of the World-Spirit. In exchange, she demanded passage with them to heal her own fractured mind. But before Kaelen could utter a word of protest, the cavern was bathed in a blinding, aggressive golden light.

Five heavily armored Inquisitors waded violently into the archive, their heavy repeating crossbows raised. The sudden, booming shout commanding their surrender echoed like a thunderclap against the stone walls. The spike of pure terror was instantaneous. Oric gasped, his massive chest heaving. The stone on his neck visibly cracked and expanded, creeping violently over his collarbone as his heart hammered against his ribs in absolute panic.

There was no time for negotiation. Moth threw herself forward, her hands plunging straight into the deepest pool of black water. She screamed, not from physical pain, but from the agonizing sensation of a memory being ripped from her consciousness. The shadows solidified into thick, iron-hard cables that wrapped around the foundational pillars of the archive. With a deafening, grinding crunch, Moth tore the pillars down. The vaulted stone ceiling collapsed in a catastrophic cascade of rubble and black water, completely sealing the passageway.

As the dust settled in the newly formed darkness of an escape tunnel, Moth collapsed onto her knees, weeping bitterly as she realized the face of her sister was completely, permanently gone. Looking down at her devastated form, Kaelen and Oric silently swore an oath to protect her, binding the three broken souls together.
