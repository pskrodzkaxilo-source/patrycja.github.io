Part II: The Cinder Wastes

Introduction Chapter 1: The Wastes Conclusion

The Cinder Wastes were a monument to man's hubris, a sprawling, hostile desert created years ago by an arcane bomb that Kaelen himself had been ordered to detonate. The environment was an absolute nightmare of sensory overload. The ground was not made of sand, but of jagged, translucent black glass that crunched sickeningly beneath their boots. Deep, jagged fissures crisscrossed the glassy terrain, sporadically erupting with towering geysers of unstable, purple Aether-fire.

The journey was taking a catastrophic toll on Oric. The extreme heat, the physical exertion of trudging over broken glass, and the constant, unpredictable explosions of purple fire kept him in a state of perpetual anxiety. His heart rate refused to slow. The calcification had spread aggressively. His entire left arm was now fused into solid, heavy stone. His only solace, his only anchor to life, was the small glass terrarium hanging at his hip. He stared through the glass at the tiny blue butterfly, forcing his lungs to expand and contract in rhythm with the gentle beating of its wings.

Suddenly, a low, mechanical growl vibrated through the glass beneath their feet. Bursting through the thick curtain of falling ash came the Inquisitor vanguard. They rode heavily armored, mechanical steam-hounds—monstrosities of brass and iron. They had tracked the magical resonance of Kaelen's gauntlet.

The sudden, ear-splitting shriek of a steam-hound caused Oric to stumble. He fell hard onto his knees. He clutched his chest as another wave of grey stone rapidly bloomed across his ribs, locking his breathing muscles. He was suffocating beneath his own turning flesh. Kaelen charged the mechanical beasts, drawing the blazing heavy sword, the Sun-Brand, in wide, devastating arcs. But a volley of crossbow bolts rained down, and one tore through Kaelen’s thigh, dropping the swordsman to the glass floor.

Oric watched from his knees, gasping for air. He reached down to grip the terrarium, desperately needing to calm his racing heart before the stone reached his lungs. But a stray steel bolt from an Inquisitor’s crossbow struck the terrarium directly. The thick glass exploded into a thousand glittering shards. The delicate blue butterfly, Oric's singular focal point of peace, was crushed effortlessly beneath the heavy, iron-shod boot of an advancing Inquisitor.

Time ground to a halt. Oric stopped fighting the curse. He let the rage consume him. A primal, earth-shattering roar tore from the giant's throat. He surged to his feet, ignoring the grinding of his stone joints. He embraced the absolute, feral fury of a man who knew he was trading his life for theirs. He charged the Inquisitors with terrifying kinetic force.

With every massive, thumping heartbeat, more of his flesh died and hardened. He slammed his good fist into the chest of an armored soldier, the impact launching the man thirty feet through the air. As his fist connected, the skin on his knuckles, wrist, and elbow instantly solidified into grey basalt. He grabbed a mechanical steam-hound by its brass jaws, ripping the machine's head off with bare hands. His entire torso calcified into thick, impenetrable rock armor.

As the last surviving Inquisitor fled in terror into the ash, Oric’s movements slowed. His heavy, stone-clad foot took one final step forward before freezing completely. The grey color rushed up his neck, over his cheeks, and across his forehead. Oric stood completely motionless, a two-ton, intricately carved statue of a roaring giant, permanently frozen in his final act of violent protection. Kaelen and Moth stared at the monument of their friend. Using the last remnants of Moth's magic and Kaelen's agonizingly burnt strength, they wove a harness of thick shadows around the stone giant, preparing to drag him across the endless glass desert.
